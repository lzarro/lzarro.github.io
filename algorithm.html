Bouman's Algorithn

Katie Bouman was a 29 year old grad student at MIT when she lead a team of 200 people to creating the algorithm that was able to generate the photo of the black hole.

No single telescope could be use to capture this image, so eight telescopes were set up all over the country. These telescopes were set up to take images of the black hole and save them as data. This data was stored in hundreds of hard drives all over the country, and sent to Bouman's team. The team's job was to read the data off of the hard drives and translate it to an image. Bouman's algorithms created multiple assumptions about the data on the hard drives and create the images when those assumptions are met.
